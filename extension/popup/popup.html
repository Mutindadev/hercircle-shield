Digital Safety Guide

Stay Safe Online

Digital violence is spreading rapidly and can have severe offline consequences. Here's how to protect yourself:

Recognize Digital Abuse
  • Online harassment - Threatening or abusive messages
  • Cyberstalking - Persistent unwanted contact
  • Image-based abuse - Sharing private images without consent
  • Deepfakes - AI-generated sexual content
  • Doxing - Sharing personal information publicly

If You Experience Abuse
  1. Block and report the abuser on the platform
  2. Save evidence - Take screenshots of messages
  3. Tell someone you trust
  4. Seek help from support organizations

Remember: You are not alone. Digital abuse is never your fault.

Your Legal Rights

You have the right to live free from violence. Here are your protections:

Global Rights (UNICEF, UN Women)

Every woman and girl has the right to:

  • Protection from gender-based violence in all its forms
  • Access to justice and legal recourse
  • Support services including healthcare and counseling
  • Economic empowerment and freedom from financial abuse
  • Education about rights and safety

Kenyan Legal Protections

Sexual Offences Act 2006

"The Act criminalizes rape, gang rape, sexual assault, defilement, and sexual harassment. Convicted rapists face a minimum sentence of 10 years, with a maximum of life imprisonment."

Key Provisions:

  • Minimum sentences for sexual offenses
  • Free medical care for survivors in public institutions
  • Protection of vulnerable witnesses
  • Prohibition of harmful cultural practices
  • Sex offenders register
Protection Against Domestic Violence Act 2015

"Domestic violence includes physical harm, sexual violence, economic abuse, emotional abuse, stalking, and harmful cultural practices such as FGM and forced marriage."

Your Rights:

  • Protection orders to prevent further abuse
  • Compensation for injuries and financial loss
  • Counseling services for survivors
  • Third-party reporting - Anyone can report on your behalf
